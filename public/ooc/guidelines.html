Roleplay Guidelines

How to use the InCharacter Commands

Whenever you put a number DIRECTLY AFTER the command, you're specifying range, some commands have fixed range, some others don't

  • /talk: Automatically used whenever you use the letter T to speak, has a spherical range of 15 tiles, it's overriden by /say so don't use /say
  • /y: Yelling, has a range of 70 blocks so it's always supposed to be yelling at full strength
  • /w: Whispering, has a range of 3 blocks, people are only going to hear if they are directly next to you
  • /em [range]: Emote, takes a range, it's usage is pretty open, from applying a 70 range to flipping the bird to someone at the other side of the battlefield, to applying 3 range to ERP. Minimum range is 3, maximum is 70. It can be used to speak (Raising your voice but not reaching full yell)
  • /ooc: Speak globally and out of character
  • /spy [name] [s/w/y]: ONLY USABLE BY ROGUES. Impersonates someone else and replicates either "talk", "whisper" or "yell"
  • /aspy [name] [range]: ONLY USABLE BY ROGUES. Impersonates someone else and replicates emoting

How to interpret certain situations

Some situations in roleplay might need some additional flavor to them and the usage of imagination

  • Building: Even if characters need to build, because this is minecraft, it's roleplayed that they are "overseeing a group of contractors/servants, etc"... Unless the character states otherwise with roleplay
